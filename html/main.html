Analysis and Implementation of DropConnect Networks

Bill DeRose & Daniel Weinand

Introduction

A common problem associated with neural networks (and learning algorithms in general) is that they perform much better on training data than testing data. When a neural network over-fits, the neurons learn the training set by rote rather than gradually adapting and learning to generalize from the input features.

The Dropout model proposes to alleviate the issue of overfitting by randomling ''dropping out'' some of the activation in each layer during training (Hinton et al.). The dropout serves to anonymizes the data and make over-fitting much more difficult for the network. In fact, training with dropout prevents complex co-adaptations that arise in the training data. By removing each neurons reliance on the other layers, each neuron on its own must retain more information. The result is a robust and efficient way to perform model averaging with neural networks (Hinton et al.).

Our final project revolves around a generalization of Hinton's Dropout, called DropConnect. In this approach, random connections between neurons are dropped between layers rather than random activations (Wan et al.). We call DropConnect a generalization of Dropout because Dropout is a special case of DropConnect: i.e. when we drop all the weights connecting a neuron in layer i to a neuron in layer i+ 1.
